About African Studies

African American Studies is an interdisciplinary academic field that explores the history, culture, politics, and experiences of African Americans and the African diaspora. It delves into the systemic structures of racism, the legacy of slavery, and the ongoing struggles for civil rights and social justice. Key topics include the transatlantic slave trade, Reconstruction, the Harlem Renaissance, the Civil Rights Movement, Black feminism, and contemporary issues like police brutality, mass incarceration, and the Black Lives Matter movement. The field highlights the contributions of African Americans to arts, literature, music, politics, and science, while also emphasizing the resilience and creativity of Black communities in shaping U.S. and global history.
